The athletes who redefined what the human body can ask of the ocean — and what the ocean demands in return.

— The Legends

HALL OF FAME

KELLY SLATER
USA
11× WSL World Champion

The greatest competitive surfer of all time. Slater holds the records for both youngest and oldest world champion — a career spanning four decades that redefined every metric of excellence in professional surfing.

LAIRD HAMILTON
USA
Pioneer of Tow-In Surfing

Co-inventor of tow-in surfing at Jaws in 1992. Hamilton's August 2000 Teahupo'o ride — known as the Millennium Wave — remains the most referenced single surfing moment in history.

BETHANY HAMILTON
USA
Symbol of Resilience

At 13, she lost her left arm in a shark attack. Less than a month later, she was back in the water. Bethany Hamilton returned to competitive surfing and became one of the most recognized athletes in the sport's history.

JOHN JOHN FLORENCE
Hawaii
2× World Champion

Two consecutive world titles in 2016 and 2017. Florence combines power surfing with aerial mastery at a level few athletes have matched. Raised on the North Shore of Oahu, he is considered the complete surfer of his generation.

MAYA GABEIRA
Brazil
Women's Big Wave World Record

Holds the Guinness World Record for the largest wave surfed by a woman — a 73.5ft face at Nazaré, Portugal. After a near-fatal wipeout at the same break in 2013, her return and subsequent record define what the sport calls courage.

ROB MACHADO
USA
Style Icon of the CT Era

The defining stylist of the 1990s Championship Tour. Machado's approach — flowing, deliberate, almost meditative — served as counterpoint to the power revolution around him and influenced a generation of progressive surfers.

— Milestone Moments

THE DEFINING MOMENTS

1992
Tow-In Surfing Invented

Laird Hamilton and Buzzy Kerbox pioneer tow-in technique at Jaws, Maui. The ocean's largest waves are suddenly accessible.

1997
Slater's Double Record

Kelly Slater becomes simultaneously the youngest and oldest world champion — a paradox only possible across a multi-decade career.

2013
WSL Goes Global

The Championship Tour expands to a fully global format. Surfing's elite compete across 10+ stops on five continents.

2020
Olympic Debut

Surfing debuts at the Tokyo Olympic Games. A sport born in the Pacific reaches its largest audience in history.

2024
Maya Breaks Her Own Record

At Nazaré, Maya Gabeira surpasses her own women's world record — the most dominant athlete in big wave history.

— Top Competitions

THE EVENTS THAT DEFINE THE SPORT

WSL CHAMPIONSHIP TOUR
Global · 10+ Stops

The world's top 34 surfers compete across more than ten Championship Tour events each season. Locations span Portugal, Brazil, Australia, Hawaii, and Tahiti — each break presenting a different test of the complete surfer.

BIG WAVE TOUR
Invitational · 4 Venues

Held across Nazaré, Jaws, Mavericks, and Pe'ahi — but only when swell conditions meet the minimum 30-foot threshold. The Big Wave Tour rewards patience, readiness, and the ability to perform at a moment's notice when the ocean delivers.

RED BULL CAPE FEAR
Invitational · Tasmania

Held at Shipstern Bluff on Tasmania's remote southeastern coast. The wave — a mutating, step-mouthed slab breaking over a submerged rock shelf — is considered the most dangerous competition venue in the world.

EDDIE AIKAU INVITATIONAL
Waimea Bay · 30ft+ Only

The most prestigious invite in surfing — held only when Waimea Bay reaches 30 feet or higher, a threshold met fewer than ten times in the event's history. Named for the legendary North Shore waterman who was lost at sea in 1978.
